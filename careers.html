WETT Certified Technician

Solid fuel inspection and evaluation role. Requires valid WETT certification and professional reporting standards.

Core Responsibilities

  • Inspect and evaluate solid fuel–burning systems (wood stoves, fireplaces, chimneys, connectors, venting)
  • Perform WETT inspections (Level 1 / Level 2 / Level 3 — as certified)
  • Document deficiencies, clearances, safety risks, and recommended corrections
  • Produce clear written reports suitable for homeowners, insurers, and real estate transactions
  • Maintain a clean, professional presence on-site

Requirements (must-have)

  • Current WETT certification (proof required)
  • Minimum 2 years practical experience with solid fuel systems OR verified inspection volume
  • Valid driver’s license and reliable transportation
  • Strong written communication and documentation skills
  • Ability to work in attics/roofs/variable conditions safely (role dependent)

Assets (nice-to-have)

  • Experience producing insurance-ready / realtor-ready inspection reports
  • Experience with cottage/rural properties and older installations
  • Comfortable coordinating with other trades on remediation scopes

313D Refrigeration & A/C Systems Mechanic

Experienced HVAC/R role focused on professional diagnostics, service, and clean finished installs.

Core Responsibilities

  • Diagnose, service, and repair HVAC/R equipment (residential/light commercial—depending on scope)
  • Perform maintenance, system checks, and professional troubleshooting
  • Communicate findings clearly to customers and document work completed
  • Maintain clean work standards and protect customer property

Requirements (must-have)

  • Valid 313D certification (proof required)
  • Minimum 3 years field experience post-certification
  • Valid driver’s license and reliable transportation
  • Ability to work independently, manage time, and communicate professionally
  • Own basic hand tools suitable for service work

Assets (nice-to-have)

  • Experience with rural/cottage service calls
  • Comfortable with documentation, photos, and clean “finished” presentation
  • Any gas-related licensing or experience (do not specify in application if not applicable)

Licensed Gas Technician (G2 / G1)

Experienced residential gas technician focused on clean installs, service calls, troubleshooting, and professional customer interaction.

Core Responsibilities

  • Install, service, and troubleshoot residential gas appliances and piping systems
  • Perform generator hook-ups, BBQ lines, appliance connections, and system diagnostics
  • Conduct safe testing, leak checks, and proper documentation
  • Communicate clearly with homeowners and provide professional recommendations
  • Maintain clean, organized job sites and protect customer property

Requirements (must-have)

  • Valid G2 or G1 gas technician license (proof required)
  • Minimum 3 years field experience (G2) or 5+ years (G1 preferred)
  • Strong knowledge of residential gas code and safe installation practices
  • Valid driver’s license and reliable transportation
  • Own professional-grade hand tools suitable for service work

Assets (nice-to-have)

  • Experience with generator installs and outdoor gas lines
  • Experience working in rural or cottage environments
  • Strong troubleshooting and diagnostic ability
  • Comfortable working independently and managing daily schedule

Note: We run a professional operation. Applicants may be asked for proof of certification, references, and a clean driver abstract.
